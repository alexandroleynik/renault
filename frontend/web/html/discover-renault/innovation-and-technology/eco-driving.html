    Eco Driving

    Simple principles before and during your trip.

    • Eco Mode
    • R-LINK & ECO DRIVING
    • Electric Vehicles

    Manage Hills

    As you descend, take your foot off the accelerator pedal, do not try to accelerate uphill whilst you are in gear.

    Acceleration and speed control

    Accelerate freely at low speeds, and remain within the speed limit when possible to allow fuel economy.

    Optimise your vehicle

    Regular monitoring of tyre pressures and a well maintained vehicle are the key to manage fuel consumption.

    Opt for a smooth ride

    Anticipate by looking at the traffic conditions in advance, to avoid hard acceleration or sudden braking.

    Adapt your gear changes

    To consume less you must be in the highest gear as soon as possible.

    Vehicle Thermal Management

    Start your journey without "warming up" the engine unnecessarily, and switch off the ignition when you stop for more than a few seconds.

    Eco Mode

    Boosting fuel economy by up to 10%.

    The ECO function is present for all new generation engines, the driver delegates to the car some of the "driving style". Driving becomes softer with longer acceleration. The ECO mode is activated by pressing a button, it disconnects during strong acceleration if needed.

    This technology changes the management of the engine torque, power and acceleration as well as the automatic air conditioning and heating.

    R-LINK & ECO DRIVING

    Training you to gradually drive more economically and environmentally.

    In the R-Link vehicle menu you can find a trip quantitative and qualitative assessment report. It evaluates the performance of your eco-driving through four ratings: an overall score out of 100 called "Eco-scoring" and three more specific notes: acceleration, gear changes and anticipation.

    Eco-coaching provides the driver with personalised advice in line with the four ratings explained in the report. The goal is to help the driver to make gradual changes towards driving more economically.

    Electric Vehicles

    Optimise energy, maximise range.

    Speed Limit

    Try pressing the accelerator pedal depending on the terrain of your journey.

    Anticipate

    Consider using energy recovery and limit braking by using engine braking.

    Create thermal comfort

    Think charge. Pre-warm or cool your vehicle's interior.

    Limit acceleration

    Adopt a flexible approach without pushing acceleration beyond the actual needs.

    Charging and warming

    The electric vehicle driver has a specific version of the Eco driving programe to maximise its range before, during and after the journey.

    Before starting the journey, the driver can start the preconditioning of the vehicle easily via various interfaces (mobile phone, computer, tablet ...) and charge the battery of the vehicle.

    Eco² Driving

    There is specific Eco driving in R-Link for electric vehicles

    Trip balance, Eco-Scoring and coaching in R-Link

    The details shown are the driver's driving record, as in conventional vehicles. (Link "combustion vehicles") Eco-coaching, offers the driver personalised advice based on the results of Eco-scoring.
